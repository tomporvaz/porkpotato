Yurts - Mongolian Cloud Houses

Once upon a time, I built a yurt. It was a great project in which I excercised my research skills, forced my self to learn woodworking, and lived outside as much as I could. I pitched a nomadic round tent for at least two winters, and in the pine barrens while attending Stockton University.

The yurt was cozy and warm. With a woodburning stove inside, temperatures easily became to hot even with freezing weather outside. The rain beat a song on the canvas roof, and the 16 feet in diameter provided just enough space for one or two.

The yurt moved on to greener pastures then its storage location in my parents garage. The yurt moved up to New Hampshire where it served as freinds forrest home. I am proud to know that it lived on in northern winter conditions.

One day I will build a another yurt with more sophisticated woodworking techniques, and vinylized canvas on the roof. I feel like the yurt needs to live up to its nomadic heritage with a design and a use case that takes advantage of its inherent mobility. Currently, I dream of a yurt as a home away from home, close to the ground, the sky and the wild!
